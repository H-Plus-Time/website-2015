A data hack for medical
research problems

Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane & Perth

23 - 25 October 2015

A FREE weekend dedicated to solving problems that medical researchers face,
whether you have a challenge yourself or want to donate your skills to the hack.

Got a medical research problem that you want to solve?

Drop us a message with the details
and we’ll get back to you.

Have a skill you’d like to donate to the hack?

Whether you’re a developer, UX designer, data analyst or researcher, we want you.

HealthHack is a product-building event that runs from a Friday evening through to Sunday. Teams work on problems that have been submitted by Problem Owners - typically medical researchers, medical organisations, hospitals or government— but they could come from anyone who has a health-related problem they want to solve. The HealthHack kicks off on Friday 24th October at 6pm and continues all day and night on Saturday 25th concluding in the evening on Sunday 26th. Festivities wrap up on the Sunday at 4pm with judging and prizes.

Congratulations to our 2013 winners

First prize - Gene Machine

Working demo Team page

A tool allowing researchers to easily and effectively assess genetic screening data, rapidly identifying anomalies and problems and expediting the refinement of clinical tests.

Second prize - EPI View

Working demo Team page

An application that biologists can use to load genome data files to visualise the entire genome in a Circos plot.

‘Spirit of HealthHack’ prize - Paperboy!

Working demo Team page

An app enabling researchers to plot academic paper search results on a heat map of the world, indicating volume of output based on university location.
